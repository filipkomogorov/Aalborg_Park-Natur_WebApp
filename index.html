Egholm

  • Length: 2.3/5.3/7.6/8.8 km
  • Estimated time:1/2.5/3.5/4 h
  • Location:Aalborg

Egholm is Denmark’s largest ecological island. It only takes a few minutes to be sailed with the ferry to Egholm from the ferry berth in Aalborg West. The ferry calls at Egholm close to Kronborg Skov, restaurant Kronborg and a larger living area with tables, benches, a fireplace and a packed lunch house. There is also a primitive campsite that is suitable for kayaking.

The island's agricultural areas are operated organically and thus the municipality's citizens and institutions have the opportunity to buy local organic goods in the center of the city.

There are many other exciting activities underway for a more sustainable local community, including composting and non-toxic gardens.

Activities

The Troll Arrow A Thousand Tongues

In 2020, the artist Thomas Dambo, together with a team of volunteers, built a giant troll of recycled wood in a secret place on Egholm. Try to find the troll using the online treasure map www.trollmap.com or ask for on the island.

Søholt cottage colony and school service

In connection with Kronborg Forest, there is also a small summer house colony, called Søholt, and a teaching pavilion, where Aalborg Municipality's school service teaches saltwater biology.

History

The first written source about Egholm is found in King Valdemar Sejr's land register of the Crown's estate from 1231, where the island is called "Æggiæholm, a house". Egg eggs can probably be attributed to the fact that the local population in the Middle Ages collected large quantities of eggs - especially gull eggs. "A house" can be interpreted as the king's hunting lodge and that the king used Egholm for hunting when he visited North Jutland

Several wild plants on Egholm are so rare that they are included on Denmark's national red list of rare and endangered plants. At Egholm's uncultivated areas, there are over 20 known habitats for the rare, original Danish rose species long-stemmed filtrose (Rosa tomentosa). Egholm's filtrose is only known from quite a few other localities in Denmark.

The island's salt marshes on the north and south coasts contain re botanical sights and rare plants. Here, plants such as medicinal cochlear, evening primrose, wing-seeded hind knee, beach tree garden, densely flowered membranous goblet, tangurt, beach daisies and the rare meadow cluster screen grow.

Both in spring and autumn, large deposits of many exciting migratory birds can be experienced on Egholm's shallow grounds, e.g. light-bellied barnacle geese, whooper swans, mallards, mallards, scallops, cormorants and spoonbills. Likewise, large flocks of vultures, vipers and starlings can be experienced on migration during the same periods.
In recent years, rare barn owls have been registered in Egholm By and on several of the island's farms. Several barn owls on winter migrations from northern Scandinavia also prefer Egholm as a hunting and living area. The owls benefit from the island's populations of field mice, shrews and boars.

On beach meadows and along the island's many ditches and drainage canals, the beach toad lives, which is a totally protected toad species. In May and June, the croaking of beach toads can be heard at dusk. At the Nature Base, field registrations can be seen in 2020, which show great progress for the species on Egholm. In recent years, a fine population of wild game has been established on Egholm with about 50-100 animals. From the dike at Egholm Hage, there are great chances of getting grazing deer in the binoculars. Foxes, hares, hedgehogs, ermine and even otters can also be experienced on the island with a little luck.


Yellow route: Walk along the municipal road or the beach to the island's westernmost vantage point. The trip is 8.8 km and can be completed in approx. 4 hours.
Red route: Walks along Nørredyb to Låen, which today is a drainage canal. Previously, here was a 60-70 meter wide water area that divided the island into two parts. The trip is 2.3 km and can be completed in 1 hour at a leisurely pace.
Blue route: Walks along Nørredyb to Egholm Hage. The trip is 7.6 km and can be completed in approx. 3.5 hours.
Green route: Walks along Nørredyb to Egholm By. The trip is 5.3 km and can be completed in approx. 2.5 hours.

Østerådalen Nord

  • Length: 1km/2km/4.1km
  • Estimated time: 0.5h-2h
  • Location: Aalborg, 3km from Center

Welcome to Østerådalen Nord, large, protected nature area only 3 km from Aalborg Centrum. That’s the perfect place to spend some time outside if you don’t want to get far from the city. Here you will find an absolutely fantastic nature and recreational opportunities in the middle of Aalborg, Østerådalen is a popular excursion destination that is visited every year by many thousands of guests who use the area for walking, running or cycling.

Østerådalen Nord also have a Health track: The health track in Østerådalen starts and ends at Infohuset's board wall. Here are boards with maps, instructions and tables about fitness. The trail is 2.3 km and marked with red arrows on black poles.

Activities

Free fishing place:
A stretch along Lergraven has been laid out as a free fishing place. It only requires a valid fishing license if you are between 18 and 65 years old.

The info house in Østerådalen
The info house is located in Østerådalen Nord with a driveway from Over Kæret in Aalborg. Throughout the year, you can experience the house's outdoor poster exhibition here and get inspiration for trips on your own in Østerådalen.

In Østerådalen there are many different plant communities, eg reed forest, elm grove, forest, wet and dry meadows, each with their very characteristic species associated. In the reed forest, the poisonous bittersweet night shadow is seen between the reeds. Along roads and ditches are many of our common flowering plants, such as field thistle, stinging nettle, gray horsetail, reindeer fan and yarrow.

Yellow foal is among the early spring flowers. In the beginning you only see the flowers, the large heart-shaped leaves do not appear until May. This is followed by a wealth of other spring flowers such as wart root, dandelion and red dandelion. Wrinkled rose is one of the summer's character plants. The fragrant petals have been used as a fragrance and flavor additive since ancient times. The plant's fruits - rose hips - are harvested in the autumn. The fruits contain large amounts of vitamin C and are a popular touch in modern Nordic cuisine.

Østerådalen's varied nature forms the basis for a rich wildlife. The wetlands attract several different bird species - both breeding and migratory birds. Many swans and geese arrive here from northern Scandinavia, Russia and the Arctic as winter visitors.

In winter, the area is visited by several species of birds of prey. Most notable is the mouse wake, which is often seen sitting on a pole while scouting for mice. Some old high-voltage masts have been preserved. Nest boxes have been set up here for the area's breeding kestrel.

Summer is the time of the insects. Most striking are the many great dragonflies as they hunt across the water or patrol back and forth along roads and paths. In late summer, swallows and starlings gather in large flocks and spend the night in the reed forest around Lergraven.

Here you have 3 hiking routes, which under normal weather conditions are wheelchair accesible:

Yellow route: "Around Østerådalen". The trip is 4.1 km and can be completed in less than 2 hours.

Blue route: The “Indkilderuten” starts from the car park at Indkildevej. The trip is 1 km and can be completed in 1⁄2 hour.

Red route: The “Bird Route” goes out to the bird sanctuary and the bird tower at Lergraven. The trip is almost 2 km.

Østerådalen Syd

  • Length: 5km / 6.5km
  • Estimated time: 1h / 1h15min
  • Location:Aalborg

Østerådalen Syd extends from Indkildevej in the north and south to Dall Møllevej between Svenstrup and Ellidshøj. There are several entrances to the valley, among others. a you can use parking spaces on Dallvej and at Skudshalevej.

Østerådalstien through Østerådalen Syd is approx. 61⁄2 km long. In several places, footpaths have been established where you can walk around the area on foot. From Dall Møllevej by Svenstrup, there is a connection to Guldbækstien - a 5 km long footpath along Guldbækken to Godthåb.

Activities

On foot and by bicycle: Østerådalstien is a continuous cycle and walking path from Dall Møllevej in Svenstrup to Aalborg Centrum via Østerådalen Nord. Part of the Østeråd Valley coincides with Hærvejen - a national and international cycle route.

Riding: It is allowed to ride on the marked riding track at Finstrupgård Ridecenter.

Canoeing: You are welcome to go canoeing on Østerå. Use the parking and canoe space at Dallvej.

Angling: In two places along the Østeråen, the municipality has designated free fishing spots Here you can fish freely, it only requires a valid fishing license if you are between 18 and 65 years old.

History

Following a successful nature restoration project in Østerådalen Nord, an initiative was taken in 1995 to continue the nature restoration to the south. Today, the entire Østerådalen appears as an important green connection that stretches from the arable land in the south towards Aalborg Centrum.

In the meadows west of Dall Villaby there is a large population of the beautiful orchid - flesh-colored cuckoo grass. At Finstrupgård Kilde- væld there is a very special plant life that is connected to the source's natural conditions, i.a. watercress, where the leaves can be used as an edible herb.

Yellow foal is one of the earliest spring flowers in Østerådalen. This is followed by a wealth of other spring flowers such as wart root, dandelion and cress. In the swampy areas along the river you can experience yellow iris, meadow watercress, and light reed. Alm. shelf grows everywhere in the river valley and is known for its fragrant flower displays in early summer and the edible, vitamin-rich berries in autumn.

As a significant feature of nature, in the southern part of the Østerå valley, the river winds its way through a mosaic of moist meadow areas. The wet meadows in particular attract many birds to the area - both breeding birds as well as migratory and winter visitors

During the winter, the area is visited by various birds of prey. Most notable is the mouse warbler, which is often seen sitting on a pole while scouting for field mice and boars.

By the river you can experience the water starling, our only diving sparrow.By lakes and ponds, the summer is seen through a bustling insect life. The great dragonflies hunt smaller insects above the water surface, which are patrolled by attentive skaters who also attack any prey of appropriate size.

Bjergbanestien

  • Length: 14 km
  • Estimated time: 5h
  • Location: Between Svenstrup and Nibe
  • Fishin is allowed
  • Moped is not allowed
  • Dogs must be led on a leash

Bjergbanestien is a local cycle route and walking path from Svenstrup via Oldstien to Godthåb and Nibe. The project to establish a cycle and walking path on the disused railway line and prepare information has been created in a collaboration between the Danish Society for Nature Conservation and Aalborg Municipality.

The section was named named Bjergbanen, as the height difference between the low-lying cities Aalborg / Nibe, and the heights above sea level at Sønderholm, was certainly not without significance when the railway was built.

History

The line was inaugurated in 1899 and existed for 70 years. It departed from the station in Svenstrup to Nibe through the stations in Godthåb, Tostrup and Sønderholm. The railway had only one track, however, two trains in different directions could pass each other at stations with yielding tracks. After many struggles with the railway itself, railway closed in 1969. The function of the railways was overtaken by development, and transport and transportation now took place by road.

The mountain railway path goes through an open, large hilly landscape, which was especially shaped during the last ice age. The ice and meltwater formation of the landscape took place over a landscape of large limestone hills. It can be clearly seen in the hills by Tostrup

The landscape is characterized by cultivated fields with grain and rapeseed as well as by agriculture with large flocks of dairy cows on grass in the summer months

The plants along the path contain many species. Within a shorter distance there is great variation between the species. Some thrive at the top of the railway dam, where the shard under the railway sleepers has created a very dry soil.

Because of the very clean and rich in oxygen water in Hasseris and Binderup, it is an ideal living condition for a large number of animals and insects. Several species of beetles, houseflies and spring flies have been found, which are only found in streams with fine water quality.
If you are lucky you might see an otter!

Mulbjerge

  • Length:
  • Estimated time:
  • Location:Aalborg

Did you know that Mulbjerge was once an island far out in the Kattegat? That the area today offers rare plants - and many different species of birds? That herring was sold to the king here? Follow the suggested tour around, get the answers and more to know. On the trip there are stories on each of the 6 stops.

Even if you move in a protected area with public access, be aware that the ground is privately owned. Setting up tents and using open fire is not allowed. Pay attention to nature and let the flowers stand. Pay special attention to birds at the shallow areas. Large flocks of birds are easily frightened. Dogs must be kept on a leash and the distance to cattle must be at least 50 m. All traffic is at your own risk, also on areas with grazing animals.

Activities

Start the trip below Stejlgabet. Here at the old fishing village there was a lot of activity years ago. The road through Stejlgabet made fishing possible and for many years large fish markets were held on the beach meadow, so traders flocked to it. The herring in particular, which then disappeared from the Sound, was so in demand that a herring industry was started. Even the king's people came twice a week and bought herring for the people of the navy and for the court. You can still see the old fishing village.

Dokkedal Backwards towards the slope between Mulbjerge and Kattegat you see the coastal country with grasslands, salt marshes and small bogs. From the coast, a valley cuts through Mulbjerge to the town of Dokkedal. The valley is like a gap in the landscape. Hence the name Stejlgabet. On the sides grow helmets and sea buckthorn. Climb Gulhøj Sailing river Bakkeø If you choose to take a small detour from the proposed route, you can climb Gulhøj 43 m.o.h., the highest point of the southern part of Mulbjerge. Cattle graze on the pasture. The view is fantastic over the Kattegat to the east and Vildmosen to the west.

History

In the exhibition building, exciting communication boards give you insight into the wildlife on the coast, the time with fishing and the area's very special cultural history. Here you can also grill and eat at the covered tables. The very long bathing bridge takes you so far beyond the beach reefs that you can swim in the deeper water. This whole area is an obvious place for smaller children to search for seashells, snail shells, small animals and aquatic plants. You can also fish and maybe catch hornfish or sea trout.

Meadows The beach meadows and the shallow water off the coast constitute an internationally very important pantry for resting water and swimming birds during the migration to Northern Europe and Siberia. In the spring, the rare light-bellied tortoiseshell, hundreds of black ducks, eider ducks, velvet ducks, gray ducks, shorebirds, great sparrows and more stop in between. Also starlings, ring doves and swallows can pass in large numbers.

Poulstrup

  • Length: 2.5km - 6.3km
  • Estimated time: 1h - 3h
  • Location: 12km south of Aalborg

Welcome to Poulstrup Lake and Dall Hede, the nature area around Poulstrup Lake is an ideal excursion destination. The area can be reached from the east via Visse or Nøvling. If you come from the west, drive via Poulstrup or Ferslev.Some text goes here

Park & Nature has marked three hiking routes in the area. In addition, a marked riding trail has been laid out. The tours are marked with black poles with color circles for each tour route, and the riding trail is marked with riding pictograms. There are good accommodation options by the lakes, but it is allowed to settle everywhere in the nature area. When driving in the fenced areas, consideration must be given to the grazing animals

You are welcome to collect mushrooms, berries and flowers for your own consumption.

Activities

Here, nature guests from horseback can experience all the different types of nature that the area offers. For the sake of the other guests, riding should take place on the marked riding trails. In several places there is the possibility of primitive spring riding./p>

Free fishing spot: The water quality in Poulstrup Lake is completely dependent on very expensive plankton in the water, for example daphnia, because these small aquatic animals by eating the water's phytoplankton, help to ensure a clear and clean water. Shells and carousels eat large amounts of zooplankton and thus degrade the lake's water quality.

History

Southwest of Aalborg Municipality's areas you will find Svalebakken, a burial mound that testifies to settlements in the area dating back to the Bronze Age 3,500 years ago. Until the middle of the 20th century, the area appeared as extensive heathland, as a result of centuries of grazing and depletion of the land. At the request of Aalborg's then postmaster, J. B. Nico-laisen, the municipality bought parts of the scenic hill area in 1946, including Poulstrup Lake. The area quickly became a popular excursion destination, and in a former country estate a tractor place was arranged according to a completely new concept, called "cafeteria".

The varied landscape offers a number of different plant communities, each with their own characteristics. Cattle grazing of the overgrazing provides a great wealth of species with character plants such as sand beard, heather, Wavy pile, broom, mountain reed warbler and blueberry.

Juniper is a character plant in light-open areas that has been uncultivated for a long time. On south-facing hills, the tuber grows meadweed and wild thyme. Forest cuckoo lily and spotted cuckoo grass can be experienced in some places. The area's forests consist of tree species such as oak, beech, rowan, thirst, birch, beaver aspen, spruce and Norway spruce.

LThe area's many habitat types provide a rich and varied wildlife. In water lakes and ponds, many aquatic insects thrive, such as gold-forged larvae, dorsal swimmers and swirls. In the lakes, the Gray-throated Diver, the Greylag Duck, the White-throated Dipper and the Green-legged Reedbird breed. In the spring, the wetlands are visited by frogs, toads and salamanders in large numbers. Mågesø and Poulstrup Sø are also night hunting reindeer for water bats.

In the very varied forest vegetation, buzzards, night owls, kestrel, great plaice, plaice and a number of different small birds breed. The open areas are habitats for song larks, thornbirds and yellow sparrows. Lizards and - more rarely - vipers can be seen sunbathing on the slopes. At dusk you may be lucky to experience foxes, badgers and deer. Of smaller mammals, there are bream, house martens, ferrets, leeches, hedgehogs, squirrels and several species of mice.
